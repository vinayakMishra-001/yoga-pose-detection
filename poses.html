T Pose
Tadasana Variation · Reference Pose
98% Accuracy Beginner Calibration Pose
Angle Detection Rules
Both Elbow Angles
> 160° (arms straight)
Both Shoulder Angles
75° – 110° (T-shape)
Both Knee Angles
> 160° (both legs straight)
Health Benefits
Excellent calibration/reference pose
Opens chest and shoulder joints
Improves posture awareness
Stretches arms and spine
Used in 3D animation rigging
Performance Tips
Stand straight with feet hip-width apart
Extend arms fully at exactly shoulder height
Keep palms facing downward
Look straight ahead, chin parallel to floor
Hold steady and breathe evenly

96% Accuracy Intermediate Standing Pose
Angle Detection Rules
Both Elbow Angles
> 160° (straight arms)
Both Shoulder Angles
70° – 115° (T-shape)
One Knee (straight)
> 160°
Other Knee (bent)
95° – 135°
Health Benefits
Strengthens legs, ankles, and feet
Stretches hips, groin, and shoulders
Improves stamina and concentration
Opens chest and lungs
Stimulates abdominal organs
Relieves backache during pregnancy
Performance Tips
Keep front knee directly over ankle
Arms fully parallel to the ground
Shoulders pressed down, not hunched
Gaze over your front fingertips
Hold for 5 steady breaths each side

Standing Knee
> 160° (leg straight)
Bent Knee
25° – 50°
Health Benefits
Improves balance and stability
Strengthens thighs, calves, ankles
Stretches inner thighs and groin
Builds mental concentration

Performance Tips

10° – 60° (arms at sides)
Both Knee Angles
> 160° (legs straight)
Health Benefits

Chair Pose
Utkatasana · Fierce Pose
93% Accuracy Intermediate Strength Pose
Angle Detection Rules
Both Elbow Angles
> 160° (arms straight up)
Both Shoulder Angles
140° – 180° (raised overhead)
Both Knee Angles
80° – 130° (bent, sitting back)
Health Benefits
Strengthens ankles, thighs, and calves
Stretches shoulders and chest
Stimulates the heart and diaphragm
Reduces flat feet and tones spine
Builds lower body endurance
Performance Tips
Keep knees together, do not let them splay
Raise both arms straight overhead
Lift your chest — do not collapse forward
Sit back as if into an invisible chair
Hold for 5–10 deep, steady breaths

Hand Up Pose
Urdhva Hastasana · Upward Salute
88% Accuracy Beginner Standing Pose
Angle Detection Rules
Both Elbow Angles
> 160° (arms fully straight)
Both Shoulder Angles
> 140° or < 30° (arms raised high)
Both Knee Angles
> 160° (legs straight)
Health Benefits
Stretches the belly and spine fully
Improves digestion and posture
Tones the upper arms and shoulders
Opens the chest and lungs
Energises and warms up the body
Performance Tips
Extend both arms fully overhead
Keep elbows straight, not bent
Reach up through your fingertips
Engage your core for stability
Keep legs straight and strong
